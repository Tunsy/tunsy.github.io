Famine Games
Famine games is an online 3D king of the hill style deathmatch game. Up to 8 players are pitted against each other in an arena with no weapons and armor. Players have to find items and treasure chests randomly generated around the map in order to arm themselves against each other. Points are gained by killing each other or capturing the control point. The first person to reach the preset number of points wins!

This game was created mainly as a learning experience for me and my team. I have always encouraged my team to push our limits and learn new things, so taking on a 3D project seemed like the logical next step after our two previous 2D projects. I was heavily involved in the weapon system, level design, and networking. I also had to learn how to work with and teach netcode to my team. This project proved to be both very ambitious and difficult, but my team and I learned a lot from it.

You can check out our GitHub repository here!

Skills used: Unity, C#
Vroom is a virtual reality drivers ed simulator created in less than 24 hours for HackDavis 2016. The goal was to create a drivers ed test as accurately as possible by simulating the use of traffic signals, stop signs, pedestrians, turn signals, mirrors, and even obstacles like other cars, the curb, or parking. Our hope was for driving students to use it to develop muscle memory for driving in a safe environment by using our makeshift gas pedal from a rock band drum kick pedal, and a steering wheel controller. I was heavily involved in teaching my team how to use Unity, programming the traffic and car physics, and level creation.
